The Deities

A few of the gods worshipped at Hyderabad's temples.

Lord Sri Rama
The seventh avatar of Vishnu, embodiment of dharma and righteousness.
Lord Venkateswara
Form of Vishnu, also called Balaji. Presiding deity at Tirumala and Chilkur.
Lord Hanuman
Devotee of Sri Rama, symbol of strength and unwavering devotion.
Goddess Mahankali
Fierce form of Shakti. Worshipped during Bonalu, a festival local to Hyderabad.

Birla Mandir

Lord Venkateswara
Built entirely of white Rajasthani marble on Naubat Pahad hillock. Completed in 1976. Offers a panoramic view of the city and Hussain Sagar.
Timings: 7:00 AM – 12:00 PM, 3:00 PM – 9:00 PM (daily)
Location: Naubat Pahad, Khairtabad
Rush time: Evenings & weekends

Chilkur Balaji Temple

Lord Balaji (Venkateswara)
Known as "Visa Balaji". One of the oldest temples in the region. The temple does not accept any monetary donations and has no VIP queue.
Timings: 5:00 AM – 8:00 PM (daily)
Location: Chilkur Village, ~30 km from Mehdipatnam
Rush time: Fridays & Sundays
Official website →

Jagannath Temple

Lord Jagannath, Balabhadra, Subhadra
Built in 2009 by the Odia community in Hyderabad. Modeled after the Puri Jagannath Temple. Famous for its annual Rath Yatra.
Timings: 5:00 AM – 1:00 PM, 3:00 PM – 8:00 PM (daily, may vary)
Location: Banjara Hills, Road No. 12
Rush time: Weekends, Rath Yatra

Karmanghat Hanuman Temple

Lord Hanuman
An ancient temple dating back to the Kakatiya era (around the 12th century). Known for the powerful Hanuman idol in a seated posture.
Timings: 6:00 AM – 1:00 PM, 3:00 PM – 8:00 PM (daily)
Location: Karmanghat, near LB Nagar
Rush time: Tuesdays & Saturdays

Peddamma Temple

Goddess Peddamma (Shakti)
A revered temple in Jubilee Hills. The Bonalu festival here draws huge crowds. "Peddamma" means "mother of mothers" in Telugu.
Timings: 5:30 AM – 1:00 PM, 4:00 PM – 9:00 PM (daily)
Location: Jubilee Hills, Road No. 55
Rush time: Sundays, Bonalu (Jul-Aug)

Sanghi Temple

Lord Venkateswara
Sits on Paramanand Giri hill, about 35 km from Hyderabad. The Raja Gopuram is visible from a distance. Architecture inspired by Chola and Chalukya styles.
Timings: 8:00 AM – 1:00 PM, 4:00 PM – 8:00 PM (daily)
Location: Sanghi Nagar, Hayathnagar
Rush time: Weekends

Ujjaini Mahankali Temple

Goddess Mahankali
The most famous Bonalu temple in Hyderabad. Located in Secunderabad. Lakhs of devotees visit during the Ashada Bonalu month.
Timings: 4:30 AM – 1:00 PM, 3:00 PM – 9:00 PM (daily, may vary)
Location: Secunderabad, near General Bazaar
Rush time: Bonalu (Jul-Aug), Sundays

Keesaragutta Temple

Sri Ramalingeswara Swamy (Lord Shiva)
A hilltop temple about 35 km from the city. Believed to have been visited by Lord Rama. Maha Shivaratri here is celebrated on a massive scale.
Timings: 6:00 AM – 12:45 PM, 3:00 PM – 7:30 PM (daily)
Location: Keesara, Medchal-Malkajgiri district
Rush time: Mondays, Maha Shivaratri
Heads up: Temple timings can change on festivals, eclipses, and special pujas. Before you visit, check with the temple directly or call ahead. The official website link above (Chilkur) is the safest source.

Hindu Festivals — 2026 Dates

Major festivals celebrated across Hyderabad's temples this year.

Makar Sankranti

January 14, 2026 (Wed)

Marks the sun's transit into Capricorn. Celebrated as Pongal in the south.

Maha Shivaratri

February 15, 2026 (Sun)

The night of Lord Shiva. Devotees fast and stay awake. Keesaragutta and other Shiva temples are packed.

Holi

March 4, 2026 (Wed)

Festival of colours. Holika Dahan is observed the previous evening.

Ugadi

March 19, 2026 (Thu)

Telugu and Kannada New Year. Chaitra Navratri also begins on this day.

Sri Rama Navami

March 26, 2026 (Thu)

Birth of Lord Rama. Big celebrations at Bhadrachalam and at Rama temples across Hyderabad.

Hanuman Jayanti

April 2, 2026 (Thu)

Birth of Lord Hanuman. Karmanghat Hanuman Temple is especially crowded.

Bonalu

July – August 2026

A festival unique to Telangana, dedicated to Goddess Mahankali. Celebrated through the month of Ashada.

Krishna Janmashtami

August 2026 (date varies regionally)

Birth of Lord Krishna. ISKCON temples celebrate with midnight aartis.

Ganesh Chaturthi

September 2026 (Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi)

Hyderabad is famous for the Khairatabad Ganesh — one of the tallest idols in India.

Sharad Navratri & Dussehra

October 11 – 20, 2026

Nine nights of Goddess Durga, ending on Dussehra (Vijayadashami) on October 20.

Karva Chauth

October 29, 2026 (Thu)

Married women fast for the well-being of their husbands.

Diwali

November 8, 2026 (Sun)

Festival of lights. Five-day celebration: Dhanteras (Nov 6), Diwali (Nov 8), Govardhan Puja (Nov 10), Bhai Dooj (Nov 11).

About these dates: Hindu festival dates follow the lunar calendar and can shift by a day depending on the regional Panchang. The dates above are based on widely-accepted 2026 Panchang sources but may differ slightly by region. For Bonalu and Janmashtami the exact dates depend on local tradition — check with your temple.

When It Gets Crowded

General patterns to plan around. These are not real-time crowd levels — just typical patterns based on temple traditions and what regular visitors report.

Day / Time What to expect Level
Weekday mornings (6 – 8 AM) Quietest time. Best for a peaceful darshan, especially at Birla Mandir and Chilkur. Low
Weekday afternoons (12 – 3 PM) Most temples close for some hours. Plan around this. Closed
Weekday evenings (5 – 8 PM) Steady flow. Aarti times can get a bit heavy. Medium
Saturdays Busy across the board. Hanuman temples especially. High
Sundays The busiest day at most Hyderabad temples. Long queues at Chilkur. High
Tuesdays Hanuman temples (Karmanghat, Akashpuri) see big crowds. High
Mondays Shiva temples like Keesaragutta get heavy traffic. Medium
Festival days Expect very long waits — sometimes 3+ hours. Maha Shivaratri, Bonalu, and Sri Rama Navami are the most crowded. Very High
Quick tip: If you can only go on a weekend, arriving right when the temple opens (5 – 6 AM) usually means you're out before the rush starts.
